Introducing BIBFRAME

BIBFRAME -- for Bibliographic Framework -- is an effort by the Library of Congress to replace MARC21 and MARCXML with a new Linked Data-based vocabulary for bibliographic description.

Visualization {# END Jumbotron Column Row DIV#}

Background

BIBFRAME, short for Bibliographic Framework, is the work of the Library of Congress's Bibliographic Framework Initiative. This initiative started with a press release by the Library of Congress in November 2011. Since that time, a formal vocabulary was released in January 2013, followed most recently with a release of an important document, BIBFRAME Use Cases and Requirements.

Creative Work

A resource reflecting a conceptual essence of the cataloging resource. (source: bibframe.org)

BIBFRAME provides specific types of Creative Works, such as Book, Serial, Article, Manuscript, MovingImage, NonmusicalAudio, Musical Audios.
