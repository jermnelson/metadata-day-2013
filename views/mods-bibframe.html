MODS to BIBFRAME

While there does not currently exist a formal MODS-to-BIBFRAME mapping at bibframe.org, a demostration of what this mapping could look like has been implemented in Redis Library Services Platform as one example of a common metadata format.

MODS

Here is MODS snippet of a student-submitted thesis in Colorado College's Digital Repository at http://hdl.handle.net/10176/coccc:7874. 

<mods:mods xmlns:mods="http://www.loc.gov/mods/v3">
  <mods:abstract>Since the earliest days of motion 
  picture, product placement has been a part of the film industry. 
  Product placement, or brand integration, is the inclusion of 
  specific brands into a creative work... 
  </mods:abstract>
  <mods:note type="admin">Colorado College Honor Code upheld.</mods:note>
  <mods:note type="thesis">Senior Thesis -- Colorado College</mods:note>
  <mods:note type="thesis" displayLabel="Degree Name">bachelor</mods:note>
  <mods:note type="thesis" displayLabel="Degree Type">Bachelor of Arts</mods:note>
  <mods:note type="bibliography">Includes bibliographical references</mods:note>
  <mods:note displayLabel="Dataset Abstract"></mods:note>
  <mods:note displayLabel="Dataset Information"></mods:note>
